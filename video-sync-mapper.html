Video Sync Mapper

Load a video into each side and find the linear time mapping that lines them up. The two clips can be different files, or the same file loaded twice (for example to sync two play-throughs of one scene). Describe each side with a t0 (a timestamp where a chosen shared moment appears) and a playback rate. The sides then map onto each other by t_right = t0_right + (rate_right / rate_left) · (t_left − t0_left). Tune the numbers by hand while linked, or unlink, line up two matching moments, and let the calibration solve them. Click a focused video and use ←/→ to nudge (hold Shift for a bigger step).

"set" stores the current playback position as that video's t0. Rates may be any nonzero value; only positive rates between 0.0625 and 16 play back smoothly, but the mapping math accepts any value.

Two-point calibration

Uncheck "Link", align each video on the same shared moment, and capture a reference point (this sets both t0 values). Then align on a second shared moment and capture it. "Solve rates" keeps the left rate fixed and computes the right rate so both points line up.
